AECHO V2 Help - ODF Display Area

<Return to Help's Home Page>

The ODF Display Area is AECHO's operational hub: it is here that ODF content is displayed in its entirety, can be edited, and Rows/Records can be selected for analysis, presenting their individual Fields and those Field's values.

An ODF XML File is simply a text file, containing no formatting information beyond the use of white-space and line-breaks: it does not have any particular font, nor any of the attributes associated with a font, such as size, typeface, color, or emphasis - all such presentation details are left to a text editor. AECHO displays all of the body-text of an ODF in a single font, 10-points in size, normal weight, colored black. Section Start-Tags (Titles) are given special emphasis, by increasing their font-size by 1 point, coloring them blue, and setting their weight to Bold. All white-space and line-breaks are presented exactly as they are in the file: AECHO does not introduce soft line-breaks in the ODF Display Area, nor does it introduce indentation based on the XML structural content.

Using the ODF Font Size control, the user can set the body text's font-size to any value ranging from 7-points to 20-points (Section Titles will display at 8-point to 21-points.)

When saving an ODF back out as a file, it is written as simple text, without any formatting information. AECHO reads and writes files assuming UTF-8 encoding, which is required by the Hauptwerk ODF XML specification: this allows the easy inclusion of special-characters into text strings, such as language-specific accent marks. The user can simply paste-in desired special characters, copied from the text editor of their choice.

The ODF Display Area is normally read-only: text may be freely selected and copied using standard mouse and keyboard operations, but cannot be pasted, deleted, or altered in any other way. However, the user can enable editing by selecting menu > Edit Mode >  ODF Editing Enabled from the Menu-Bar, after which all the standard operating system functions for changing text become available, such as pasting, deleting, typing, and over-typing text.

The ODF Display Area presents both vertical and horizontal scroll-bars, as needed. If some lines of text within the ODF are longer that the available width of the area at the current font-size setting, the horizontal scroll-bar is displayed, regardless of whether or not any of the long lines are currently onscreen. AECHO never word-wraps long text lines in this display area.

Single-clicking at a spot in the ODF positions the visible Cursor/Caret to that point, updating the position information found in the Data Panel Area. Selecting text will do the same, with the Cursor/Caret position determined by the direction of the selection: if from left-to-right, the Cursor/Caret will be considered to be just to the right of the selected text; if from right-to-left, just to the left of the selected text. Note that these operations do not select nor analyze the Row/Record, nor do they update the Section position or the Section description, even if the new Cursor/Caret position is now in a different Section than before. Any position in the ODF may be safely single-clicked.

Double-clicking at a spot does all of the above, plus it selects the entire Row (1 or more Lines, depending on the content), copies the selected Row to the XML Row/Record Area, updates the current Section name and position, and loads the matching Section description into the Descriptive Text Area. If the Row is of type "Record" (bounded by '<o>...</o>' Tags), is is broken down into its constituent Fields onto the Tags Panel, and the values of those Fields are displayed alongside the short-names of the Tags. Any position in the ODF may be safely double-clicked.

The current position can be advanced or retarded by 1, 10, or 100 Lines at a time using any of the Next Ln, Prev Ln, > 10 L, < 10 L, > 100 L, or < 100 L buttons. These buttons select the new Line/Row, just like a double-click, so Section and descriptive information will be appropriately updated. These six controls position the Cursor/Caret at the beginning of the new Line. If the Row has multiple Lines, the Next Ln and Prev Ln buttons will step through the Row one Line at a time: the entire Row will remain selected as you step through it, but the current Line Cursor/Caret positioning information will be visibly updated. These positioning buttons are bounded by the beginning and the end of the ODF - they do not advance past those boundaries, do not wrap-around.
