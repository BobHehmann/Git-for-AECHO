AECHO V2 Help - Managing The Mouse: Single & Double-Clicks

<Return to Help's Home Page>

AECHO recognizes and responds to both single and double mouse-clicks (and even the occasional triple-click) in its text areas: the resulting behavior varies by area. In the XML Row/Record Area, the Search Area, and the Descriptive Text Area, mouse-clicks of any type are limited to text selection; in the ODF Display Area, in addition to selecting text, the various mouse-clicks also initiate significant functional behaviors, potentially altering the contents of many different AECHO displays and fields with a single click.

ODF Display Area
Single-Click
Positions the Cursor/Caret between two characters, at the point of the click, without selecting any text. Gives the area Focus. In the Data Panel Area, updates the fields: Line Number; Caret Position; Line Start; Line End; Row Start; and Row End. In the Data Panel Area, does not update the fields: Section Start; Section End; or Number of Tags. Does not change the current Section, not alter the XML Row/Record Area, the Tags Panel Area, nor the Descriptive Text Area. Does not alter the Status-Bar.
Double-Click
Positions the Cursor/Caret between two characters, at the point of the click, and selects the entire Row. Gives the area Focus. In the Data Panel Area, updates the fields: Section Start; Section End; Line Number; Caret Position; Line Start; Line End; Row Start; Row End; and Number of Tags. Updates the current Section to the one containing the selected Row; copies all Lines of the selected Row into the XML Row/Record Area; presents the individual Field-Tags and their Values in the Tags Panel Area, and displays appropriate context-sensitive action buttons; updates the Descriptive Text Area to match the Section; updates the Status-Bar Row Type field.
XML Row/Record Area
Single-Click
Positions the Cursor/Caret between two characters, at the point of the click, without selecting any text. Gives the area Focus. Takes no other actions.
Double-Click
Selects a single typographical word, which is bounded by non-alphanumeric characters, such as white-space, punctuation, and special characters. Gives the area Focus. Takes no other actions.
Triple-Click
Extends a double-click word selection to the entire line, without crossing over line-break boundaries. Gives the area Focus. Takes no other actions.
Search Area
Single-Click
Positions the Cursor/Caret between two characters, at the point of the click, without selecting any text. Gives the are Focus. Takes no other actions.
Double-Click
Selects a single typographical word, which is bounded by non-alphanumeric characters, such as white-space, punctuation, and special characters. Gives the area Focus. Takes no other actions.
Descriptive Text Area
Single-Click
Positions the Cursor/Caret between two characters, at the point of the click, without selecting any text. Gives the are Focus. Takes no other actions.
Double-Click
Selects a single typographical word, which is bounded by non-alphanumeric characters, such as white-space, punctuation, and special characters. Gives the area Focus. Takes no other actions.
Triple-Click
Extends a double-click word selection to the entire line, without crossing over line-break boundaries. Gives the area Focus. Takes no other actions.
