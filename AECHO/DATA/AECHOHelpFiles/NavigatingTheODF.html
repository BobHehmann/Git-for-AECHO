AECHO V2 Help - Navigating the ODF Display Area

<Return to Help's Home Page>
 
ODFs can be quite large, sometimes 100,000+ lines and 10,000,000+ characters long. AECHO provides several ways to navigate efficiently around the ODF Display Area.

Vertical Scroll-Bar
The ODF Display Area is equipped with a vertical scroll-bar, which can be used to rapidly move from one end of the ODF to the other. Using the scroll bar never changes any aspect of the current location: Section, Row, and Cursor/Caret position remain unaffected.

Single Mouse-Click
A single-click in the ODF repositions the Cursor/Caret to the point of the click, updating the Line and Row positions, but does not select the Row, nor change the current Section. Click here to learn more.

Double Mouse-Click
Like the single-click, the Cursor/Caret, Line, and Row positions are updated. Additionally, the double-click selects the entire Row, updating the XML Row/Record Area with the contents of the new Row, contextually updating the Tags Panel Area, and updating the current Section, including its descriptive text. Click here to learn more.

Menu-Bar Section Items
Every Section is directly accessible through the Menu-Bar: choosing a Section this way is the equivalent of double-clicking on the Section's Start-Tag (Title Line). Click here to learn more.

Text Searching
AECHO supports text searches both forward and backward from the current position, or forward from the beginning of the ODF. When successful, a search behaves similar to a single-click, repositioning the Cursor/Caret, Line, and Row without selecting that Row or updating the Section. It leaves the newly located text selected. Click here to learn more.

Line Positioning
The current Line can be incremented or decremented by 1, 10, or 100 lines by a single button-click. Positioning this way is the equivalent of double-clicking on the beginning of the new Line. Click here to learn more.

Markers (Bookmarks)
Up to four bookmarks may be set at one time using any of the four Marker buttons. Selecting a set Marker is the equivalent of single-clicking on the first character of the bookmarked Line. Click here to learn more.
